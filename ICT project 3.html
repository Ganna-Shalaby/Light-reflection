Light Reflection and Refraction

Steam project

Objective

The aim of this project is to demonstrate and understand the basic principles of light reflection and refraction through an experiment

Materials Required

  • A flashlight or laser pointer
  • Mirror
  • Piece of glass
  • Cardboard
  • Paper

What is Reflection?

Reflection is the bouncing back of light when it hits a smooth surface (Eg: Mirror) The angle at which the light hits the surface (angle of incidence) is equal to the angle it reflects at (angle of reflection).

What is Refraction?

Refraction is the bending of light as it passes from one transparent medium to another (Eg: Air, water or glass), each with different densities. This occurs because light travels at different speeds in different materials when each has a different optical density.

Experiment Steps

1. Reflection:

Shine the flashlight onto the mirror at an angle. Place the white paper to observe the reflected beam. Measure the angles and observe the symmetry.

2. Refraction:

After lighting a lazer pointer through a piece of glass, the direction of the glass changes.

Conclusion

This project helps us understand how light behaves when it encounters different surfaces. Reflection and refraction are key concepts in optics and have applications in lenses, mirrors, glasses, cameras, and more.
